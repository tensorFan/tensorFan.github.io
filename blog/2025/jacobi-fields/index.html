Let $(M, g)$ be a Riemannian manifold with Levi-Civita connection \(\nabla\).

Recall smooth variation $\Gamma: (-\varepsilon, \varepsilon) \times [a, b] \to M$ of smooth $\gamma: [a, b] \to M$. Denote $\Gamma_s (t) := \Gamma(s, t)$, $\Gamma^t (s) := \Gamma(s, t)$

$T := \partial_t \Gamma$, $S := \partial_s \Gamma$, $V := S|_{s=0}$. $\quad$ [C smooth variation field]

Theorem 1

Every Jacobi field $V$ along geodesic $\gamma$ is the variation field of a geodesic variation of $\gamma$.

Proof

We have just seen the variation field of a geodesic variation being a Jacobi field. $(*)$

Conversely, let $V: [0, 1] \to TM$ be Jacobi along $\gamma$. Define curve $\sigma(s) = \exp_{\gamma(0)}(sV(0))$ and vector field $W: [0, \varepsilon] \to TM$ s.t. $\begin{cases} W(0) = \dot{\gamma}(0) \ \left.\frac{D}{ds}W\right _{s=0} = \left.\frac{D}{dt}V\right _{t=0} \end{cases}$

$W$ exists by theory of ODEs in a chart.

Then $\Gamma(s, t) := \exp_{\sigma(s)}(tW(s))$

is a geodesic variation since $\Gamma_s$ are geodesics by def. of $\exp_{\sigma(s)}$.

From the first part $(*)$ we know $J := S _{s=0}$ is Jacobi.

We have: $J(0) = \partial_s \Gamma(s, 0)|{s=0} = \partial_s \sigma(s)|{s=0} = V(0)$

Since $\exp_p(0) = p$. Also:

$\frac{D}{dt}J(0) = \frac{D}{dt}J(t) _{t=0} = \frac{D}{dt}S _{s,t=0} = \frac{D}{dt}T(s,t) _{s,t=0} = \frac{D}{ds}W(s) _{s=0} = \frac{D}{dt}V(0)$

Since derivative at origin of exp is the identity.

Then $J = V$ by the following Lemma. $\square$
